Overview sales guidance

IBM SPSS® Modeler is a comprehensive predictive analytics platform, designed to bring predictive intelligence to everyday business problems, enabling front-line employees or systems to make more effective decisions and improve outcomes. Modeler scales from desktop installations through to larger deployments that are integrated within operational systems and provides a range of advanced analytics including text analytics, entity analytics, social network analysis, automated modeling and data preparation in addition to decision management and optimization.

This enables organizations to improve business processes and help people or systems consistently make the right decisions by delivering recommended actions at the point of impact. The result is a rapid return on investment (ROI) and the ability to proactively and repeatedly reduce costs and increase productivity.

Examples of user benefits include identifying which customers are likely to leave (customer attrition), predicting what products and services prospects are likely to purchase together (retail market basket analysis), identifying which claims have a high probability of fraud (insurance), mitigating risk by predicting which robots on the assembly line are likely to fail (predictive maintenance), and deploying police resources based on “hot spots” (predictive policing).

IBM SPSS Modeler is offered as four editions:

  • IBM SPSS Modeler Gold – Includes all the capabilities of IBM SPSS Modeler Premium with the ability to build and deploy predictive models directly into the business process to help people or systems make the right decisions each time. This is achieved with Decision Management which combines predictive analytics with rules, scoring and optimization within an organization's processes and operational systems to deliver recommended actions at the point of impact. Available on-premises, as hybrid or in the cloud.
  • IBM SPSS Modeler Premium – Includes all the capabilities of IBM SPSS Modeler Professional, along with a range of advanced algorithms and capabilities including text analytics, entity analytics, social network analysis to address a wider multitude of business problems and increase model accuracy with unstructured data.
  • IBM SPSS Modeler Professional – Provides a range of advanced algorithms, data manipulation and automated modeling and preparation techniques to build predictive models and uncover hidden patterns in structured data.
  • IBM SPSS Modeler Personal – Modeling workbench for the individual user with all of the base capabilities of IBM SPSS Modeler Professional, but without the collaboration, enterprise-grade performance or deployment capabilities.

All enablement materials can be found on the Modeler, ADM and C&DS Launch Enablement wiki .

Sales enablement description

Within a business environment, the main objective of analytics is to improve a business outcome such as; increasing revenue by reducing customer attrition, increasing cross sell rates through a call center, decreasing costs by identifying fraudulent claims before payment, or servicing a component in a production line to minimize downtime. Analytics can be applied to improve a decision, which in turn will result in a better outcome.

Data mining is the process of uncovering patterns in data using analytical techniques such as descriptive analysis and predictive modeling through to more advanced techniques involving text analytics, entity analytics, decision management and optimization. By identifying patterns and deploying predictive models into operational systems, organizations enable systems and people to consistently make the right decision at the point of impact The result is the ability to maximize outcomes based on the predictive intelligence hidden in data of growing size and complexity.

IBM SPSS Modeler can be adopted and installed on a variety of environments – it can be used as a pure on-premises solution, server products can be hosted to create a hybrid environment, and IBM SPSS Modeler Gold is available in the cloud (Software-as-a-Service [SaaS] model). Consider all options when working with customers and prospects.

Benefits/value proposition

What we do How we do it
First, we provide an intuitive visual interface for creating models that incorporate structured and unstructured data from a variety of data sources. Visual workbench for modeling with nodes for data access, text analytics, database access, data transformation and modeling with the ability to leverage Statistics for ad-hoc analysis and reporting. A workbench and deployment environment that is available on-premises or in the cloud.
Then, we address every aspect of the data mining effort based on a cross-industry standard process for data mining (CRISP-DM) standard. Nodes for all stages of the CRISP-DM lifecycle with example streams, based on CRISP-DM methodology and CRISP-DM guidance built-in.
Finally, we provide seamless integration and deployment of predictive information into existing business processes. Integration with Collaboration and Deployment Services provides collaboration, visibility and automation with Decision Management serving as the backbone that integrates results of models into call centers, campaign management systems and other industry and department-specific applications for real-time positive ROI decision-making. Integration with IBM Cognos® Business Intelligence (BI) ensure that users can easily mine their BI data and make results visible across the enterprise from within a single interface.

Competitive differentiation

Easy to learn/use:

  • Visual approach – no programming
  • Supports documentation and reuse of models and modeling streams
  • Flexible deployment and licensing options

Power and automation:

  • Automated data preparation and modeling
  • Full range of modeling algorithms
  • Full range of data access and data transformation functions
  • Multi model creation and evaluation (ensemble models)
  • Integrated analysis of text, web and survey data

Scalable solution with flexible deployment options:

  • No need for proprietary databases
  • SQL pushback
  • Maximized use of infrastructure with multithreading, clustering and use of in-database data mining procedures
  • Already fully integrated with additional IBM technology
  • Can be deployed on-premises, in the cloud or as a hybrid

Main Competitors

Data mining competitors:
SAS
  • Program language driven data mining
  • Proprietary platform and proprietary data format
  • Limited/poor text mining capabilities
SAP
  • Fragmented solution (HANA, SAP BO, KXEN)
  • Data management script based
  • Weak text mining and no entity analytics
Oracle
  • Fewer native algorithms supported
  • Works on Oracle data only
  • Difficult to use
KNIME/RapidMiner
  • Free-ware/low cost versions extremely limited in functionality (bait and switch)
  • Weak on performance and scaleability
  • Lack of business domain expertise
R
  • Completely program driven (is a scripting language)
  • Difficult to scale (performance and deployment are issues)
  • Can be used (and made better) with IBM SPSS Modeler
Outsourced Analytics
  • Time
  • Control over data and analysis
  • Available techniques/approaches
